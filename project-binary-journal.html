The Binary Journal

An opinion-driven technology publication built from scratch to analyze emerging products, trends, and events with clarity, structure, and original perspective.

Context

The Binary Journal was created as a platform for thoughtful analysis in the technology space. Rather than summarizing news or press releases, the goal was to provide structured opinions and deeper reasoning about how new technologies impact users, developers, and society.

Problem

Much of existing tech coverage prioritizes speed over insight, resulting in repetitive reporting with little original analysis. This makes it difficult for readers to understand the broader implications of new products or trends beyond surface-level announcements.

Solution

I designed and developed a custom website using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript with a strong emphasis on readability, content hierarchy, and navigation. The platform was intentionally built without templates to allow full control over layout and iteration.

Articles are structured to present clear arguments rather than summaries, allowing the site to function as a journal of analysis instead of a news feed. SEO was implemented to ensure discoverability while maintaining content quality.

Outcome

The Binary Journal has reached readers in over 70 countries through organic search, demonstrating effective SEO implementation and content structure. The site continues to evolve as new articles and features are added.

This project demonstrates my ability to combine technical execution, structured writing, and long-term maintenance to build a platform that communicates ideas clearly and consistently.

Tools & Skills

  • HTML, CSS, JavaScript (no templates)
  • Content structure and information hierarchy
  • Search Engine Optimization (Google Search Console)
  • Opinion-driven technical writing
  • Website maintenance and iteration
