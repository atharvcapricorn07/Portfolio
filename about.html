About

I am a student developer focused on building systems that remove friction, scale effectively, and hold up under real-world use. I’m most interested in problems where clarity, reliability, and long-term impact matter more than surface-level complexity.

My work sits at the intersection of technology, operations, and design. I’ve built AI-assisted tools, internal dashboards, and production websites for real users, with an emphasis on making systems easier to understand, maintain, and improve over time.

I approach projects by identifying bottlenecks, defining constraints early, and iterating toward simple solutions that scale. I value documentation, feedback, and ownership, and I work best in environments that trust contributors with meaningful responsibility.

I’m especially drawn to work involving accessibility, analytical tooling, and human-centered applications of AI — areas where thoughtful system design can materially improve how people interact with the world.
